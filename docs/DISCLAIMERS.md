## AI models and hallucinations

> **MaxisOS supplies instructions (prompts). It does not supply the AI model.**

Helper behavior is defined by text instructions. **Actual replies are generated by the language model** (for example, Anthropic’s Claude) at runtime. The authors do not control each token the model emits.

**What that means in practice:**

- **Models are probabilistic.** The same question can yield different answers. Some answers will be strong; some will be confidently wrong. That reflects how the technology works, not necessarily a bug in MaxisOS.

- **Models hallucinate.** They can invent facts, fake citations, bad math, or plausible nonsense. For anything that matters—dates, numbers, medical or legal claims—**verify independently.** Do not treat AI text as automatically true.

- **MaxisOS is designed and tested with Anthropic’s Claude.** Claude includes safety training and policies that usually keep behavior within reasonable bounds for these prompts. If you use another model (fork, config change, or otherwise), behavior and safety may differ. **Risk from model choice and configuration is yours.**

- **Prompt-based safety is best-effort.** Instructions may remind users to seek professional help or verify advice. Those are requests to the model, not hard guarantees. A model can disregard them.

- **No promised quality level.** Prompts have been exercised with Claude in real use, but that is not a warranty. Output varies by session, model version, and context. Testing under some conditions does not guarantee the same results for every user, every time.

- **No responsibility for model output.** The authors are not liable for hallucinations, bad advice, offensive content, or any other generated text. You are using a stochastic system; treat its output accordingly.
